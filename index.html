Adding a New Deal to Trello
Step-by-step guide for new users
1
Open the template card
In the "Upcoming" column, find the blue card at the top
Upcoming...
MASTER BOOKING DEETS TEMPLATE
■ This card is a template
DELIVERABLES
VERY LIKELY AWAITING...
Click the blue card at the top of the Upcoming column
←
It is pinned at the top of the Upcoming column. You can't miss it: it's the one with the blue "This card is a template" badge.
2
Create a card from the template
Inside the template card, click the button to create a new card
MASTER BOOKING DEETS TEMPLATE
+ Create card from template
A new card will be created with all sections pre-filled
When you click "Create card from template," Trello instantly generates a new card with all the correct sections already set up. You just need to fill them in.
3
Name the card correctly
Follow the naming structure exactly, every time
Naming structure:
(Client Name)
Project Name -
$Value
-
Initials
Example:
(Ericsson) DSP Leaders Sponsorship - $25,000 - ZC
(Ericsson)
Client name, always in brackets
DSP Leaders Sponsorship
The product or deal type
$25,000
Deal value in USD
ZC
Your initials (the deal owner)
Consistent naming keeps the pipeline readable for the whole team. If you're unsure of the exact product name, check existing cards in the board for reference.
4
Add the SoW or proposal text
Paste the scope of work or proposal into the card description
(Ericsson) DSP Leaders Sponsorship - $25k - ZC
☰ Description
Paste your SoW or proposal text here...
Click the description area to start typing or pasting
Click the description field inside the card and paste in the relevant SoW or proposal text. This keeps everything tied to the deal in one place, so anyone on the team can see the full context.
5
Place the card in the right pipeline stage
Drag the card to the correct position within the Upcoming column
Possible but unlikely
Early conversation, no budget confirmed
Low confidence; early-stage interest only
Possible
Proposal sent, good engagement
Actively progressing; real interest shown
Very likely awaiting paperwork
Verbal yes, contract being reviewed
Verbal commitment; waiting on docs
Drag your card to the section that best matches where the deal stands today
To drag a card: click and hold it, then move it up or down within the Upcoming column. Release it in the right group. The "Very Likely" section is highlighted in orange, so it's easy to spot.
6
Move sold deals to Sold/Actionable
When a deal is won, drag the card across to the right column
Upcoming...
(Ericsson) DSP Leaders - $25k - ZC
➞
Sold/Actionable
(Ericsson) DSP Leaders - $25k - ZC
✓ Deal won
Click and hold the card, drag it horizontally to the right across to the "Sold/Actionable" column, and drop it there. This signals to the team that the deal is closed and ready for fulfilment.
Done! The deal is now live in the project management board. Check in with David if you're ever unsure which stage a deal belongs in, or if a deal needs to be split across multiple products.
